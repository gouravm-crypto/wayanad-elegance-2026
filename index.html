🦅 Karapuzha Adventure Hub — 605m Zipline + Glass Bridge
The Karapuzha dam adventure zone. 605m dam zipline — you fly over the reservoir at full speed, hills and water beneath, wind slamming your face, the whole squad watching from the platform. After the flight: the Glass Bridge — fully transparent floor, suspended above the dam. Two iconic experiences in one zone, back to back. Non-zipliners have the Rose Garden and Musical Fountains running simultaneously — no one sits idle.
Call ahead and book group zipline slots — walk-in availability is not guaranteed for 16
Glass Bridge reel: walk slowly, look straight down through the glass floor, scream naturally
Harness and helmet fully provided — no one backs out, everyone flies

🚣 Pookode Lake — Mirror Waters & Pedal Boats
A natural freshwater crater lake in the heart of Vythiri forest. Mirror-still water at golden hour reflecting the entire treeline. Pedal-boating in groups of 4. The bamboo forest trail around the edge is a 20-minute loop perfect for candid portraits — soft afternoon light, no harsh shadows. The deliberate calm after the zipline thrill.
Eastern bank of the lake — the perfect mirror reflection shot at this hour

🌅 Banasura Sagar Dam — Electric Buggy Sunset
Asia's largest earthen dam. The group panoramic from the dam top is the definitive money shot of the trip. And it is five minutes from the resort — the perfect pre-check-in capstone.
Electric buggy ride itself is content — slow, elevated, scenic route to the top

6:30 AM
🫀 Chembra Peak — The Heart-Shaped Lake That Never Dries
Wayanad's crown jewel. 2,100m above sea level. The Hridayathadakam — a naturally heart-shaped lake that has never dried in recorded history, not in any drought year, not ever. 40% of the squad: the full 3.5km steep hike through tea plantations and mountain meadows. 60% of the squad: the 15-minute Watchtower walk with equally staggering panoramic views of the Wayanad valley. Everyone reunites at the base. Only 200 people per day are permitted. Pre-book tonight.
PRE-BOOK ONLINE TONIGHT — 200/day strict cap, fills completely days in advance
Heart lake = the trip's single most iconic photograph — allow 30 minutes at the lake
6 AM arrival means mist on the peaks — other-worldly conditions that burn off by 8 AM
Watchtower option is genuinely stunning — no one misses out on the panoramic

12:30 PM
🌊 Kanthanpara Waterfalls — Hidden Tea Garden Cascade
A wide, two-tier waterfall hidden in the tea gardens near Meppadi. Safe paved access path, proper viewing platform, optional dip at the base pool. Far less crowded than Soochipara or Meenmutty. The path through the tea estate before you reach the falls is nearly as beautiful as the falls itself — green rows of tea, bamboo, and the sound of water. This is the perfect post-Chembra calm.
Tea garden backdrop = the most cinematic waterfall setting in Wayanad, period
Far less crowded than the famous falls — you practically have it to yourselves
Bring a plastic bag — waterfall splash at the base will wet everything nearby
⭐ Hidden Gem₹40/person9 AM – 4:30 PMNear Meppadi
2:30 PM
🗿 Edakkal Caves — 6,000-Year-Old Rock Carvings
A natural cave cleft in the Ambukuthi hills. Inside: Neolithic and Mesolithic petroglyphs — human figures, animals, spirals, geometric symbols carved by hands that walked this earth 6,000 years ago. 45-minute climb with proper stairs and handrails throughout. Inside the cave, light filters down through the cleft. It genuinely feels like Indiana Jones. Nothing else on this trip is in the same category for sheer uniqueness.
Most unique single visual of the entire trip — nothing else competes with 6,000-year-old carvings
Handrails and stairs throughout — every single squad member can reach the carvings comfortably
₹25/person~1.5 hrs totalOpen till 5 PMAmbukuthi Hills
