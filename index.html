The Mission Vehicle

18-Seater Tempo Traveller

₹51,600. 1,600 km. 16 people. Door to door from Guindy to God's Own Country and back — together, the entire time. The overnight journey isn't a problem to solve. It's the opening act.

We booked an 18-seater for 16 people — deliberately. Two seats open means extra legroom, extra luggage space, and no one cramped on a 1,600km overnight round trip. This is what thoughtful planning looks like.

The Coimbatore route was chosen over the more direct path to avoid multi-state permit complications — Salem → Coimbatore → Gudalur → Lakkidi → Wayanad. Fewer stops at borders, smoother driver experience, and the Gudalur dhaba at 3:30 AM is a planned ritual.

The overnight journey is intentional. Bluetooth speaker on. Cards in hand. Snacks from the box. Chai at every stop. The TT isn't just transport — it's the first memory of this trip.

🫀 Chembra Peak — Heart-Shaped Lake Trek
Wayanad's crown jewel. The trek to the heart-shaped lake that has never dried in recorded history. 7km round trip, approximately 4.5–5 hours for a mixed-fitness group of 16. Everyone goes at their own pace — the group splits naturally into fast and easy hikers, reunites at the heart lake for the group photo, then descends together. Only 200 trekkers per day. Pre-book with the forest office — number is in the Bookings section.

Steel water bottles mandatory — no single-use plastic allowed at Chembra

🐘 Tholpetty Wildlife Sanctuary — Jeep Safari
Wayanad Wildlife Sanctuary at dawn. Open-top jeeps carry the squad through the forest — elephants, gaur (Indian bison), spotted deer, and if lucky, leopard tracks. This is a genuine wildlife experience, not a theme park. Animals are most active in the early morning hours. The guides know exactly where to look. Two hours inside the sanctuary — every moment is a possible sighting.
Wear muted colours — no bright red, orange, or white inside the sanctuary
Silence is the protocol — loud groups see less wildlife

🗿 Edakkal Caves — 6,000-Year-Old Prehistoric Carvings
A natural cave cleft in the Ambukuthi hills. Inside: Neolithic rock engravings — human figures, animals, spirals, symbols carved by hands that walked this earth 6,000 years ago. Safe stairs and handrails throughout the 45-minute climb. Inside the cave, light filters through the cleft dramatically. The most visually unique cultural experience in all of Wayanad. Nothing else on this trip is in the same category for sheer "I can't believe this exists."
Steel bottles mandatory — same plastic rules as Chembra apply at Edakkal
Handrails throughout — all 16 people can reach the carvings comfortably
Photography inside the cave is the highlight — the carvings in natural light
